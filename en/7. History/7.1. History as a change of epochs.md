History — for me — is an umbrella jumble that joins
1. reflections on what happens in society — in the past, present, and future
2. sociology, politics, economics, culture
How can one prove that in society one epoch replaces another?
I propose the notion of a Legalization Band.
An idea appears in the public field — and turns out to be a crime.
A public defender of this idea is punished, put under house arrest — like Galileo, burned — like Jan Hus.
But time passes.
The idea gains new defenders, and at some point the idea receives recognition and then becomes the standard.
Like barriers in developmental psychology, the legalization band proves that somewhere between the moment of appearance in the public field (and punishment) and the moment of recognition lies the point of epoch change. Before the change the idea is punishable; after that point — the idea becomes the standard

This resembles Rolle’s theorem: if a function continuous on an interval takes values of different signs at the ends of the interval, there exists a point on the interval at which the function equals zero.

The change of epochs — from the third through the 8th — is shown by the following legalization bands.

3. Ancient Empires — Sargon the Great — recognition
4. Christianity — persecuted — state religion
5. Reformation — Jan Hus is burned — Luther becomes head of a confession
6. Scientific discussion — Galileo is tried — Descartes hides — Spinoza is expelled — Newton is showered with awards
7. Non-Euclidean geometry — Lobachevsky is not heard — Riemann is scolded — Dedekind publishes Riemann — Beltrami, Poincaré, Klein, Minkowski, Einstein
8. Discourse — Galich, Brodsky, Zinoviev — they still do not hear me, but they no longer punish — the Discourse club exists
