Abraham Maslow (1908–1970).
Parents came from Kyiv to the USA at the start of the century.
Finished university, began work as Thorndike’s assistant
At that time US psychology was set by behaviorists (Thorndike, Watson) and psychoanalysts (Freud’s students).

Maslow came to psychology to study the human being.
What is main in a human? First, motivation — what a person wants, what goals they have. Second, intellect — ability to overcome obstacles on the way to those goals.

But what goals did Freud, Watson and Thorndike study?

In Freud the main human motive is sex.
Behaviorists study the intellect of rats, whose hunger.

Even if psychoanalysts and behaviorists knew all answers to their questions — what could they say about the human beyond hunger and sex?

And Maslow formulated his own research program: study the human in their higher manifestations — moral, social, creative.
True, how to study that in laboratories was unclear. But studying biographies of creative people, analyzing their lives and writing books about it was more interesting than feeding rats in a lab.

So Maslow took the first step toward a psychology that is a science of the human (not of rats and maniacs).
The second step was taken by his student Graves.
And the third we take together.
