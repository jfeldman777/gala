Consider a person who investigates something as a system.
For example they found an old bicycle design and decided to understand how it works.

Their steps.

	1) name — old bicycle
	2) Parts it consists of (wheels, handlebar...)
	3) Ways of fastening (pedals, chain...)
	4) How to ride it?

Or in our terms
1) one object (bicycle)
2) Many objects (parts)
3) One process (how to assemble)
4) Many processes (ways of riding)

 And now the four questions of the fourth rule
 1) homogeneity — find one class all elements belong to, e.g. COLORS or SOUNDS — one class — one object
 2) Completeness — a set of colors or sounds of what size? Did we see all elements? (many objects)
 3) Unique order? (One process)
 4) History of origin — a story, many processes

These four steps (object, many objects, process, many processes) — the first half of the scale Levels of the field of attention
