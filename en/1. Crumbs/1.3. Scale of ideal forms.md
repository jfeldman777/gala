Consider a pair of categories from philosophy
material–ideal.
Note that philosophy also has a similar pair
material–spiritual
and in these two pairs the word MATERIAL has different meanings.

That is one reason I do not work with a lone concept — at minimum with a pair.

So in the pair material–ideal
Material is life itself — a continuous inarticulate flow in which we all swim.
The moment we rise above that flow and try to make sense of it, describe it, tell the description to another — we work with ideal substance. Or for short — with the ideal.
What forms does this ideal take? Here is the scale of ideal forms (another row of concepts)

1. Event — something happened
2. Fact — event plus interpretation
3. Object — something or someone passing through different facts
4. Generalization ("British scientists have shown")
5. Methods
6. Values
7. Representations
8. Knowledge / Models

The first 4 forms are about the past, the second 4 about the future.

Each class has its own quality criteria. Knowledge need not be useful (a method must). Representations need not withstand strict testing (knowledge must).

Tune it a little — and you get a solid foolproofing.
