Baumeister lists five definitions of Truth he found in the history of philosophy.

Truth — what is useful.  
Truth — what everyone agrees on (consensus).  
Truth — what is obvious.  
Truth — what is self-consistent (internally non-contradictory).  
Truth — what corresponds to the real world (adequate).

Yet from his reasoning it is unclear whether these definitions must be applied at once. Or whether each has its own domain of applicability.

If the second — then which?  
If the first — then how to combine the results? They may not coincide.

And finally the main point.  
Why five criteria?  
Are others possible?  
And where did these come from?
