Do you need clarifying examples?
If not, you can skip this page.

1. Hold the term. — For example, if a definition says ONE or MANY, you can't say (ONE or SEVERAL) — that would be stepping outside our discourse, and for that space beyond the fence there's a special name: EXCURSUS.

2. The Lone Zombie — For example, you can't ask for a definition of the word MANY — but you can ask which series MANY belongs to — answer: the series ONE-MANY.

3. The task in the background. — Sometimes each participant has their own task — political negotiations are like that, for instance. It's not ideal, but it's acceptable. Much worse is when every participant has one and the same task — to show off — in which case the very call to CLARIFY THE TASK often makes participants stop thinking about their beloved selves and choose a task common to everyone.

4. Justification of the series, four questions — A good example is the seven colors of the rainbow. — Origin? Proposed by Newton. Order — unique. Homogeneity — present. But completeness is missing. For example, one could use not 7 but 256 colors — that happens too.

5. Structuring. We will inevitably run into infinite scales of values. Even the color blue can be given more shades than the eye is able to tell apart. But since we work for a person making a decision within a limited window of time and possibility, it is not just our right but our duty to replace the infinite with the finite — continuous scales thereby turn into finite, small series.

6. Reverse engineering. — Here are two books. One begins with the claim that the earth is flat, the other that the earth is round.
Do you think the first book carries false information? Not necessarily. If it only deals with the problem of how long a ball thrown from a window takes to fall to the ground, it can still be a perfectly good book.

7. Self-applicability. I'll write out the justification of the fourth rule in the next passage.

8. Openness forever. — Here's a man everyone considered bad. Then he died, his notes were published, and we all found out he'd been a good man. Is this forever? I don't know — time will tell.

I hope it's clear now.
