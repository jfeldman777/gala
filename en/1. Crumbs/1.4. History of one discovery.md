We were walking through evening Moscow and saw a huge scoreboard-TV. The TV was not very good quality; pixel cells were visible, they sometimes went dark, and I saw how because of that a line turned into a heap of dots.
It looked like a thread breaking in a necklace — beads spilling in a heap.
That is, a line — I said out loud — degrading, turns into a heap of dots. And what, degrading, turns into a line?
A bundle of lines, degrading, turns into one line

When an hour later I was home, I called EN (we lived then in different ends of Moscow) and asked:

Take a notebook and a pen — or pencil — and draw to dictation.
Yes, took it.
Draw a table of two columns and five rows. Drawing?
Drawing.
First row. One point.
Second row. Many points.
Third row. One line
Fourth row. Many lines
Fifth row. One plane

And that is all?
For now all
Five is few. There should be seven, better — eight. — she said.
I will think. — I said and hung up.

She was right. There should be eight rows.

That was February 18, 1989
