First, we must agree what knowledge of a scientific type is (in our discourse other words are used for non-scientific knowledge — if knowledge, then of scientific type)

Knowledge — in our discourse — consists of five components.
1. Problem = part of reality = the city
2. Model = ideal construction = the map
3. Task list = list of pairs (A,B), where A and B are points in the city. Task (A,B) means — using only the map, take a passenger from A to B
4. Observer = taxi driver
5. Equipment = car

Knowledge as a whole is a separate item on the scale of ideal forms.
And its components...
Problem — object.
Model —

If these components are not assembled, testing knowledge is forbidden.

How do we test knowledge? (P-Procedure)

Loop over the task list (A,B)
	1) map city points A and B to map points A1 and B1
	2) build on the map a route as map points C1, ... X1
	3) map C1... X1 to city points C,... X
	4) Using orientation A, C, ... X, B, drive the passenger from A to B
	5) Ask — is the passenger satisfied

	After the loop remains %% of trips where the passenger is satisfied = D
Set two thresholds, e.g. P1 = 95% and P2 = 50%.

If D is greater than P1, knowledge is true
If D is less than P2, knowledge is false
Otherwise knowledge is doubtful

Notes.
Adequacy of the map — possibility of forward and reverse mapping (A to A1 and C1 to C)
Self-consistency of the map — a path on the map from A1 to B1 can always be found
And if you have no observer?
Then Bishop Berkeley is right — then there is nothing.
Or in our terms ---- nothing to test and no reason to

Remember that the existence of an observer is not derived from the model and must be added to knowledge independently of the model

And what is a model? A state machine

A graph, states = vertices, links = edges, scenarios (traversals of the graph).
When a model takes part in the P-Procedure, for every pair of vertices A and B (from the task list) there must exist a traversal route starting at A and ending at B.

One model may enter different pieces of knowledge. Model quality is measured by the best knowledge it enters.
