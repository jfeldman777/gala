The following chain of ideas is supported by a few personal premises.

In Marxism (which I studied diligently at university), there is a general thesis that thinking is merely the shadow of muscular movement. My own personal thinking is strongly coupled to my muscular movement. The combination of these two circumstances pushed me toward the thesis that "in an artist's creative work, it's the eye running across the composition that thinks. The muscular movement of the viewer's eye reproduces the muscular movement of the artist's eye. And since the movement of thought is a shadow cast by muscular movement, the viewer's thinking reproduces the artist's thinking."

The pleasure taken in a picture, then, is the pleasure of a muscular movement of a certain complexity, one that matches, one to one, the complexity of the visual composition.

Suppose an era lasts as long as its main reference level stays in place.
The next era — the next reference level.
Eight levels means eight eras:

1. Hunters
2. Farmers
3. Ancient empires
4. The Middle Ages
5. Renaissance / Reformation
6. The Industrial Age
7. The 20th century
8. The present day
