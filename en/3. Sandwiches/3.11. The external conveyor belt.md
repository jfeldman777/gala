What does a student's success on an exam depend on?
We can compare different students on the same stream of tasks.
But it's much more interesting to run the reverse thought experiment.

Let's vary the exam's parameters in order to make the student's results worse.
What can we change for this?

1) Time. If you reduce the time allotted per task, a moment comes when the student can't keep up.
2) Presentation of information. If, for example, the assignment is written in a language the student doesn't know, they won't keep up.
3) Task difficulty. If you raise the difficulty, the student won't keep up.

These three groups of factors coincide with the three bottlenecks of the internal conveyor belt:
1) Attention holds complexity
2) Encodings assign new languages
3) Warm-up controls time

So, on the one hand, we now know the three most important models that we'll need to develop and describe.

And on the other hand, the parameters of these three models, measured for a specific person, task, or community, lead us straight into the virtual world.
