Culture is vast; the history of culture is enormous.
Where should we lead the child?

Shirman suggested the idea: recapitulation.

In their development from birth to adulthood, a child passes through the same phases of historical development that humanity passed through, from the moment it separated from the animal world to our own time.

How did Shirman know about this? He was involved in the Moscow historical reenactment movement. The movement was engaged in historical modeling — modern-day men learned to ride horses and fence with sabers, and modern-day women learned to sew costumes and cook the way their ancestors did in 1812, or in 1700, or at any other time.

Shirman mentioned Hall's name, but apart from the "idea of historical modeling" in its most general form, he couldn't offer anything concrete.
