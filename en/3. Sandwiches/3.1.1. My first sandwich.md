In February 1989, I discovered the staircase of levels. In May, I found levels everywhere. And I asked myself: why are levels everywhere?
And in August, I proved the Basic Theorem that answers this question.
That was my first sandwich.
I understood that this discourse would outlive me.

The second sandwich arose from a question about historical progress and its mover. And in the autumn of 1989 I showed that the rules of the elementary conflict contain enough information to see that very mover. Wrap it up.

Since then, many more medium and large discoveries have happened. As for the small ones — too many to count.
The total number of sandwiches has reached... I'll know once I finish writing the book.
