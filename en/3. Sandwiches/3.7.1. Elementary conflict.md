Imagine two sides that have met, each showing its own level (a single one). There's little time, they're about to part, but the outcome of the meeting will remain and will affect their future lives.

The rules of elementary conflict state that the outcome of the meeting is calculated as follows.

1. If the sides' levels are equal — competition, a draw, each gets 2 points.
2. If the sides' levels are adjacent — this is cooperation, the lower one gets 3 points, the higher one gets 4.
3. In all other cases it is war: the lower one attacks, the higher one dies or flees. The lower one gets +1 point, the higher one gets minus 1000.

Homework.

What strategy would you choose, if negotiating is not allowed, and levels can be any of 1 through 8? Levels are revealed simultaneously.
