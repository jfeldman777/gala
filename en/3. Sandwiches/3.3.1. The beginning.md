Moscow, 1988. Gorbachev's perestroika.

Among the Moscow pedagogical crowd, there was unanimous agreement on this point: what we teach children today is, at best, a waste of resources, and at worst, harmful to everyone involved.

So this isn't what we should be teaching. Then what should we teach?

We had a chance for a direct experiment. It happened at Tubelsky's school — and Tubelsky was considered Moscow's model, showcase democrat. He became principal in 1985, received "super-site" status in 1992, and died of a double stroke in 2007.

Two experimenters, named Khalimov and Shirman, invited us to that site. Tubelsky gave them the kids who came to school but understood nothing, forming a mixed-age group. Khalimov and Shirman thought it over and invited EN and me. By the end of the year, all the participants had caught up with their classes and returned to the regular process.
