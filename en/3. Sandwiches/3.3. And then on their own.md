We proceed from the premise that being a teacher is not a job but a mission.

So what does this mission consist of?

There is a child and there is culture. The teacher must take the child by the hand and lead him to culture. And then — on his own.

This brings to mind the fairy tale about breadcrumbs (see the beginning of the book). And it also brings to mind a scene from a film.

A boy sits on a bench and reads a book aloud. As he reads, pigeons gather around him — more and more pigeons. Having finished the first chapter, the boy stands up, leaves the book on the bench, says loudly "and the rest, on your own" — and leaves.
The camera shows the title of the book.
How to Build a Birdhouse.
And the rest, on your own.
End of film.
