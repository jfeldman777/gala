Which entities does the measure of structural complexity apply to?

We had three examples:
music, driving, hiking.
Within each example there were situations of a definite level of complexity — and there were situations of indefinite complexity.
In both cases, within a situation we would define a task — and the task's complexity level was unambiguous.

For example.
A rattle is a first-level situation. The task is also first-level.
But scales can be played in different ways (a situation), even though we picked the simplest, third-level variant.

The third link in the chain is the state of the person (who is banging a rattle or playing scales).

Situation – task – state of the person – complexity level – level of the field of attention.
