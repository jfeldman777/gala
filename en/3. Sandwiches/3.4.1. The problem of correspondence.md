I understood the coincidence of the child's and society's trajectories literally.

If there is a correspondence
age X = era X,
then, at age X, studying era X would be optimal for everyone involved.

But here's the trouble — the correspondence described by Hutchinson was no good at all. We were advised to have children dig in sand until age 5, feed pets until 12, and develop observational skill and caution until 15.
But why not cultivate observational skill and caution at age 4, and feed the animals at five??

And where, exactly, is the history of culture in all this?
Something more fundamental was needed.
And I found it.
