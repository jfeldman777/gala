The same sequence appears in a child and in ancient visual art:
1. One object
2. Many objects
3. One process
And then what?
4. Many processes
5. One plane
6. Many planes
7. One system
8. Many systems

That was the physical version of the scale. But there's also a geometric version:
	1. A point
	2. Many points
	3. A line
	4. Many lines
	5. One plane
	6. Many planes
	7. One space
	8. Many spaces

The history of visual art gives us plenty of examples where a work's composition is exactly this way — in the corresponding era.
