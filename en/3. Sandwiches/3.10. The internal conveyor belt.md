So, tasks come flying at a person, and each one requires doing something about it.

(For anyone who doesn't like this picture, here's another. We ourselves go looking for tasks, so the brain doesn't sit idle. From the inside, both versions look the same.)

So the first thing to pay attention to is emotions. They peer into the near future, evaluating tasks that haven't arrived yet but are about to.
They ask questions and answer them somehow:

Is it important?
Is it dangerous?
Is it urgent?

They also compare the difficulty of tasks with the person's capabilities, and this affects the next agent.

The next agent is called will. It takes the nearest task and must make a decision. Ignore it? Or send it for processing?

If it's decided to process the task, it goes into waiting room No. 1, where two more agents work — Attention and Encodings.
Attention copies the task into an internal copy, and this copy is written in internal languages. The Encodings agent is responsible for how the internal languages work.
This is a very important stretch, since the loss of information during copying and internal encoding is irreversible. And if we end up solving the wrong task, the answer will not be accepted.

Now the copies of the task, written in the internal language(s), enter waiting room No. 2.
To move further, the task must accumulate energy of its own type.

(Energy for solving equations and energy for playing football are not interchangeable.)
The Warm-up subsystem is responsible for accumulating the right kind of energy.

Having accumulated enough energy, the task moves to Waiting Room No. 3.
After some waiting there, the task reaches the operating room and the operating table.

Here four agents work.
Memory pulls up similar tasks, which are stored together with their solutions.
Combinatorial Intelligence tries to assemble a solution for the new task out of pieces of old solutions.
Creative Intelligence translates the task into different languages. This gives rise to various ideas.
Intuition listens to everyone else and offers them unexpected options.

If a solution is found, there's one more step — the answer must be converted into a form acceptable to the client. Attention, Encodings, Intuition, and Will all take part here.

That's how this teamwork goes.
