In Krylov’s fables (and as is well known many of them are retellings of La Fontaine’s fables, and he retold Aesop) the levels are obvious — and although the moral can be formulated without them, applying levels helps both ways — we better understand the fable and better understand the levels.

The Swan, the Pike and the Crab — 4 is needed, they can only do 3

The Quartet — again there are 2, 3, 5 — and 4 is needed.

The Monkey and the Spectacles — there is 5, and 4 is needed

The Dragonfly and the Ant — 24 is better than 15

The Pig under the Oak — 46 do not work against 12

The Cat and the Cook — 46 does not work against 12

and so on
