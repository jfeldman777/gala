It happened in April 1986 at the Chernobyl nuclear power plant. One of the units went out of control, the reaction became unmanageable, an explosion occurred. The plant was shut down. The land around for hundreds of kilometers became a zone of radioactive contamination.

How did this happen?

In the USSR there was ministry №1; it designed nuclear power plants. The staff understood how a plant works. And they wrote instructions for those who do not understand. But some cases were so rare that they were not described in the instructions. They made up the so-called “gray zone.”

In the USSR there was ministry №2. Understanding was not required of its staff. They received an object and a packet of instructions for it. They worked by the instructions.

All nuclear power plants were under the management of ministry №1. And all other power plants — under the management of ministry №2.

And then a new boss came into the Cabinet. And decided to transfer the nuclear power plants to ministry №2, starting with Chernobyl.
And transferred them.

And why did he decide that? He himself was level 5. That means theorists of 6 attached themselves to him from above (65). And specialists from ministry №2 attached themselves to him from below (45).
The second, you will agree, is much more pleasant.

So the curator removed the scientists (6) and put specialist-officials (4) in place.

And the reactor went into the gray zone (5). And in the instructions (4) there is nothing about that. And ministry №2 works by the instructions.
And now the reactor manages the people (54)

And the system went haywire.

And the boss from the Cabinet knew nothing about the gray zone. Because in ministry №1 they did not consider it anything important — after all, we cope!

The boundary between those who write instructions and those who follow them turned out to be beyond the brain that makes decisions. Why? Isn’t it obvious? There is no difference there (thought the boss). All people are the same. Anyone can write an instruction when needed. And can follow one if needed. That is reasoning. And it is more pleasant for me to work with officials than with theorists — that is feeling.

## How the theory of levels could have prevented the Chernobyl disaster.

In TUAI (the Theory of Levels of Abstract Intelligence) each job/task/activity is matched with a certain level of complexity.

If you understand what is happening, your level is 6. (Ministry №1 = person-1)

If you act chaotically, unpredictably, your level is 5. (reactor in the gray zone)

If you follow an instruction, your level is 4. (Ministry №2 = person-2)

Neighboring levels cooperate, and the senior one manages.  
Distant levels war; the junior attacks, the senior dies or flees.
