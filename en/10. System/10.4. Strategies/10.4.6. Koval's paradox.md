Rostislav Koval pointed out that if you strictly follow the rules of the elementary conflict, then neither an army nor science could exist.

For example, officer #3 would not obey general — he would attack him and the object. But that doesn't happen. Why?

Here is Koval's answer, and I agree with it. In the army, in science, and in other similar structures (church, corporation...) there exist positions that are formally vacant,

but that invite someone to occupy them temporarily.

For example, the REGULATIONS (the Charter).
Whoever reads and follows the regulations takes position 4 where everyone else is at 3 or 5 — and turns into a mediator, and the conflict disappears.

In a certain sense, one could say that the REGULATIONS are a mediator-entity.

Other entities of this kind:
the PARADE GROUND or TERRITORY in the army (2 between 1 and 3);

competitions for students — 3 between 2 and 4 in science,
grants for instructors — 5 between 4 and 6 in science.

The mafia also has its own regulations and territories, and if it doesn't, that's called lawlessness.

If a scientific community has no competitions and no grants, then it isn't science but a sect.
