If you are a lecturer
speaking to an unfamiliar audience,
try to use all the codings.

The "on every channel" principle.

Show slides, voice your commentary out loud,
write the most important points on a large screen, use images and meanings, state the scenarios.
Use schemes where you can, and pictures where you can.

If you are a student
preparing for an exam and making mistakes while solving problems, use every coding you can.

Read the problem out loud, listen to yourself reading it.
Imagine what is happening in detail.
Draw a picture.
Draw a scheme.
Write down the key words — what matters most here.

Treat one more coding as another person who will now check whether your reasoning and calculations are correct.

The more codings, the fewer mistakes.

If you are a researcher
working on a hard problem, remember —
translating the problem from one language to another is the main source of fresh ideas and solutions.
