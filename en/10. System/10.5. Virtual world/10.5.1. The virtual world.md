Modeling — from the point of view of my professors at university — is the basic form of thinking.
To make sense of something, to understand it, means to build a model suitable for calculations in specific cases.
My virtual world consists of entities of three types:
1. People
2. Tasks
3. Communities

The main thing in this world is the meeting of entities.
- a person meets a task
- a person meets a community
- a person meets a person. This case has a special name, the Problem of Dialogue.

To calculate the outcomes of meetings, I use passports.
We used to use a blank form for passports.
![[кл.png]]--
Here there are 23 parameters; each one (with a specific entity in mind) must be assigned a numeric value of 0, +1, or -1.

Now I am also adding to the passport
Contexts, divided into 6 layers.
A context shows what one needs to know in order to make contact with a given entity.
Contexts correspond to layers of being, which in turn correspond to time horizons and strategies in culture.

|     | layer of being | time horizon | strategy in culture |
| --- | -------------- | ------------- | --------------------- |
| 1   | Emotional      | Minutes       | Outsider              |
| 2   | Material       | Days          | Consumer               |
| 3   | Social         | Years         | Transmitter            |
| 4   | Cultural       | Generations   | Author                 |
| 5   | Spiritual      | Millennia     | Wanderer               |
| 6   | Cosmic         | Millions of years | Invader           |
