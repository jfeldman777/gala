
When we are faced with an entity from the virtual world and must fill in its passport by looking at its real-world prototype, we are entitled to find markers in real objects (in their description), or to interpret the descriptions so that markers appear.

We are not obliged to find all the markers — one or two is enough, as long as they capture the essential properties of the real object.

Here are some more examples.

Engels:
Freedom is a recognized necessity.
What is this about?
Freedom = 5
Necessity = 3 (a force compels)
Recognized = 4 (someone explained to us that it can't be otherwise)

The author claims that 5 can be replaced by 3 and 4 without loss of quality — but this is a false claim.

Another example:
War is peace, Freedom is slavery (Orwell).

Distinguishing (peace from war, freedom from slavery) is a level-2 operation.
Identifying them is level 1.
The author invites us to step down from level 2 to level 1 — to simplify.

The expressions
Might makes right
The end justifies the means
are a victory of level 3
over levels 6 (justifying) and 4 (rights, rules).
