A meeting between two people reveals the fact that contexts cannot be ignored.
After this discovery, we will have to reconsider the other two kinds of meetings as well.

So, first the two lists of contexts are compared (one from each side).

What are the formal conditions under which contexts do not stand in the way of further rapprochement?

Here is the general idea.
Take two contexts from the same layer.
They may contain sensitive points.
For example, there may be a painful topic (the war in Ukraine, and two people with opposite positions meet).

There may be several such points at once.
This could be xenophobia, and so on.

For a task, this might be a mandatory license requirement; for a community, a credit history, and so on.

A person familiar with the current situation can easily write down the possible sensitive points for all participants. Their number is finite — this is a fixed list, the second sheet of the passport.

The map (of a person, a task, a community) is the first sheet.
