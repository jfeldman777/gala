But can we calculate how the population is distributed across these groups?
In this section I'll offer some thoughts on that.

I will rely on two studies.
Everett Rogers (1961) and Geoffrey Moore (1991).

First I'll describe what the studies are about, then I'll add my own model, and finally I'll try their numbers against our concepts.

Rogers studied the diffusion of innovations in agriculture in the US.
Moore studied the market, hardware, and software.
Moore's contribution is the concept of the Chasm — it's easy to sell something new to group #2, but after that you need to change strategy.
