In history, every cross-section has its own description in levels.

If you take one level, you can define an era, or a single group that sets the standard during that era.

Two consecutive levels — power (in Europe).
Three consecutive levels — an economic order.
Four consecutive levels — stylistic quartets — set a life stance (a civic position).

These are large groups in society that we easily recognize.

There are also currents in art — they are defined by sets of different lengths.

For example, Baroque, Classicism, and Romanticism can be distinguished as:

45 (Baroque)
46 (Classicism)
56 (Romanticism)

And Tradition–Modernity–Postmodernity–Metamodernity are:

34 – 367 – 457 – 3568
