Temperament is a macro-characteristic of a person; it can be identified through years of observation at close range. (Hippocrates–Galen)

Warm-up is a micro-characteristic of a person, determined by observation over the course of an hour under specially created conditions. (Feldman)

A person's character is a collection of their traits.
Character traits come in two kinds.
First, typical reactions to typical circumstances.
Second, typical states of mind.

One person is always polite with people, another is rude — these are typical reactions.
One person is always cheerful, jokes, and laughs; another is quiet and thoughtful.

Hippocrates (an ancient Greek physician, 400 BC) discovered, and Galen (an ancient Roman physician, 200 AD) retold in his surviving works, that people fall into four temperaments.

I will show that the properties of temperaments grow out of the warm-up curves of childhood under naive upbringing.
