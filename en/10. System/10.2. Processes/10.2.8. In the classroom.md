We have seen how features of the brain shape self-esteem and character traits. Another example like this shows how the joint influence of warm-up and codings in the learning process shapes a value system and an attitude toward morality.

Suppose there are 24 people in a class, one temperament each, and one line of codings evenly assigns one of three codings as each person's native one.

Each cell has 2 people in it.

Four clusters, A, B, C, D, are in different conditions.

How does the lesson proceed?

The teacher — in our example a text-type sanguine — throws out problems one at a time. Everyone solves them individually. The teacher grades the solved problem and gives the next one.

So, what grades do the students get?

Two people from cell C match the teacher in coding and warm-up; they have just the right amount of time, they are the STRAIGHT-A STUDENTS. In life, they will be confident that whoever works honestly will be rewarded and will live a worthy life.

12 people from cells A are the LOSERS. Their warm-up is slow and they can't keep up with the teacher no matter how hard they try. In life, their main goal is not to slide into failing grades — they don't ask for the stars from the sky either.

8 people from cells B are fast in warm-up. But since they have to translate information into an internal form and back, they don't keep up. Yet outside school and at home they manage fine. So they are not willing to accept the role of a C-student. How can they keep up? Solving honestly is not an option, but they can cheat (CHEATERS — bribe-givers). But who will let them copy?

In cells C and D there are two people who work faster than the teacher, and they have two strategies.

Strategy C: be a straight-A student, follow the rules, help the teacher.

Strategy D: help the cheaters, let them copy in exchange for certain perks. This is also a CHEATER, but of a higher rank — a bribe-taker.

All these students will soon step into adult life: 50% losers, 1/8 straight-A students, and the rest cheaters (3/8).
