Hippocrates (460–370 BC)
Galen Claudius (129–216 AD)

We know about Hippocrates's temperaments from Galen's works; the psychological descriptions apparently belong to Galen.

(Compiled at my request by ChatGPT)

**1. Sanguine**

- Cheerful, sociable, active.
- Gets excited quickly and calms down just as quickly.
- Switches easily between tasks.
- Shallow in feelings.
- Inclined to optimism.

**2. Choleric**

- Quick-tempered, energetic, decisive.
- Reacts quickly and is prone to intense emotions.
- A natural leader, but can be aggressive.
- Unstable in mood.
- Stubborn and strives for dominance.

**3. Phlegmatic**

- Calm, balanced, slow.
- Restrained in emotions and actions.
- Reliable and stable.
- Dislikes change.
- Takes a long time to get into a new task, but works persistently.

**4. Melancholic**

- Withdrawn, impressionable, anxious.
- Prone to deep feelings.
- Sensitive to criticism.
- Has creative thinking.
- Often prone to despondency.
