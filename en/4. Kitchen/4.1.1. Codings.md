Imagine the Brain as a computer; inside it — specialized processors for different kinds of information

This is how I picture it.

There are three processing lines; in each line the processors partly compete, partly complement or replace one another

The lines are specialized. The first line processes small units of information, the second — medium ones (made of small ones). The third — large ones (made of medium ones)

In the first line
hearing — sight — touch and muscle sense — taste — smell.

In the second line —
text — picture — diagram

In the third line
Images — meanings — scenarios

In each row there are strong, medium, and weak processors

A picture remembers every detail; a diagram simplifies at once and remembers the minimum. Text remembers a textual description; in detail it sits between picture and diagram

Images–meanings–scenarios needs justification. The first (image, object) — meaning (relations between objects, many objects) and scenarios (traversal of a graph — one process — many processes)

composite encodings:
metaphor — an image conveys a meaning
diagram — scheme and picture
table — structure and text
heuristic — scenario and meaning
