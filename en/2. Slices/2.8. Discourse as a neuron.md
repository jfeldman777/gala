There was a life. In that life there were several zones — small zones, where I sensed problems and tried to formulate a solution. At first there were only three zones.
1. School. Why does it make everyone feel bad.
2. History. Marxism tried to model it — unfortunately, unsuccessfully.
3. Philosophy itself. Marxism is fading, leaving an empty space.
It turned out that these three problems could be formulated in one and the same language.
That's how a single center appeared — the body of a neuron — and the three zones turned into attachment points, synapses.

Gradually it became clear that three synapses weren't enough; at least nine were needed — five domains and four facets (FFHA — the four-faceted human activity).
![[карта002 1.png]]Now let's see how this system should function according to its purpose.
People with their problems arrive at the synapses (zones). Nine zones are enough to cover any specialization. There, in the nine zones, work experts who were trained at the center.
At the center itself work the keepers of the discourse.
There's one more factor. We called it earlier the excursus.
Here's the thing. Experts communicate with the center through pipelines — dendrites.
The dendrite's sheath serves as insulation from the outside world and from other discourses.
If the sheath loses its integrity, foreign bodies penetrate inside and the discourse starts to get sick. Special people — defenders — deal with this problem.
So a discourse needs
keepers, defenders, and experts. It also needs a system for training all three groups, plus market research and the recruitment of new clients and staff.
