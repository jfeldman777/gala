You're a caregiver, you have a group of children in your charge.
They keep starting up different activities.
Let's agree right away: you don't kill all their activities, and you don't force them to keep doing yours.
That you rely on their actions and their energy.
How do you act so that the system doesn't spin out of control (which is possible)?

The general rule is this.

If a child starts an even-numbered activity (we're playing, we find it interesting), then by definition it isn't destructive. Here you can help, or you can choose not to respond at all (if you don't have the resources for it).

See "The Experience of Difficult Victories."

But if a child starts an odd-numbered activity, you must respond.

1 — I want, 3 — I'm first, 5 — I'll take the risk, 7 — I have a brilliant idea.

You need to respond with plus one — the next even level.

I want — this is allowed, that is not (1–2)
I'm first — besides you, there are others too (3–4)
I'll take the risk — but have you thought it through? There are options with no risk at all (5–6)
I have a brilliant idea — but here are its long-term consequences (7–8)
