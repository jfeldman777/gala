But what is the project method? Here's AI on that.

The project method appeared in the **USA at the start of the 20th century**.

- Its roots go back to the philosophy of **John Dewey (1859–1952)** — an American philosopher and educator, a proponent of "learning by doing."
- His student and colleague, the educator **William H. Kilpatrick (1871–1965)**, published a work in **1918** called _"The Project Method,"_ which was the first to describe this approach in detail and give it its name.

It is Kilpatrick who is usually called the author of the project method, with Dewey as its intellectual inspiration.

The method reached Russia in the 1920s and was actively introduced in schools (under the name "project method" or "problem-based learning method"), but in 1931 it was officially banned as "not corresponding to the tasks of the school." Its return came only in the 1990s, when the emphasis was again placed on students' independent work. (AI reference note)

However, there are nuances. 1) Dewey and Kilpatrick emphasized the difference between school tasks and real tasks. 2) Where do real tasks come from? The teacher organizes the process so that real tasks arise on their own, and the children only take on solving them and see them through to the end.

Here
- the teacher distinguishes the educational from the real
- the teacher organizes the environment
- the children take on the task and see the solution through to the end
That is, Dewey and Kilpatrick place the emphasis on the second level for the teacher, and the third for the student.
