Imagine a planet where there is only one round ocean along the equator, and everything else is land.

The ocean is philosophy.
The land is where people far from philosophy live — living ordinary lives.
The land here is called Cosmos.

In the ocean there are islands. On each island lives a philosopher with his students. They develop their own version of philosophy — their own language, or discourse.

Between the islands, teachers of philosophy, their students, and various enthusiasts sail across the sea.
These ships and all their activity are called Excursion.

They sail from island to island, copy various pieces of various discourses, and build beautiful constructions out of them.

Their role is twofold. For some discourses, this is a chance not to sink into oblivion.
For others — a spoiling of treasures.

Is there logic in the excursion? Different logics get mixed together in it, which means there is no logic there at all.

It seems to me that Hegel's teaching on dialectical logic (see, rule six) was made for exactly this case.
