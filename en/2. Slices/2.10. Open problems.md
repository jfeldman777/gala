This term is printed on the flyleaf of the book "The Philosopher on the Beach."
![[Pasted image 20260708150950.png]]Closed problems are ones whose answer is in the back of the problem book.
Semi-open problems are ones where roughly how to solve them is known, but you have to do the counting yourself, down to a number.
Open problems are ones nobody knows how to solve. Moreover, nobody even knows what should count as a solution to the problem and what shouldn't.

The levels — the numbers on the left — are for those who understand them.
