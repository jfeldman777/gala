Here are listed indicators that people actually apply, not always in the right place.

1)      Success = good (a dubious criterion — see technical approach 1. Counterargument — we may fail to see the main task and solve a secondary one)

2)      If we know what the task will be, then signs of the right level = good (an unreliable criterion)

3)      The higher the level, the better (a false criterion). The correct variant — level according to place

4)      Individualism = evil, collectivism = good. A false criterion. The correct one — “after every odd level a higher even one should keep watch”

5)      War = evil, cooperation = good, competition = compromise. (a good criterion)

6)      Service and goal levels. Where goal levels stand above service ones — good; where below service ones — evil. A dual personality and a measure of good — when there are several levels. (a good criterion)
