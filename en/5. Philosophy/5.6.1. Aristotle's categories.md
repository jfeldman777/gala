
Aristotle treats concepts as generalizations of real features of real objects. He notes that some concepts enter into others. The deeper (more abstract) the concept, the more abstract the concept linked with it

According to Aristotle there exist 10 concepts that can no longer be generalized — so fundamental are they.
Aristotle calls them categories (ultimate concepts)
These are
1. Substance
2. Place
3. Time
4. Position
5. Quantity
6. Quality
7. Having
8. Relation
9. Action
10. Passion (being-affected)

This row ought to be justified.
