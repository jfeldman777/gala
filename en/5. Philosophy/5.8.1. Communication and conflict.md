## Theory of communication

> **Communication — transmission of a message in order to align pictures of the world or joint actions of the participants.**

A message has two limiting types:

1. **Signal** — aligns action in a short time window.  
    Example: the wave of a conductor’s baton.
    
2. **Fragment of a picture of the world** — must be built into the recipient’s picture of the world.  
    For full understanding, neighboring parts of their picture must be compatible with the sender’s picture.
    

Understanding is not “yes/no,” but a quantity from 0 to 1. It is measured by:

- coincidence of the models constructed;
    
- correctness of the action;
    
- ability to draw the expected inference or forecast.
    

Every communication has temporal parameters:

- time allowed for a reply;
    
- the message’s period of relevance;
    
- duration of the consequences of the reply;
    
- cost of correcting an error.

## Theory of conflict

> **Conflict arises when participants must align actions but use incompatible pictures of the world, goals, rules, or ways of encoding the situation.**

Main sources of conflict:

- different pictures of the world;
    
- different goals;
    
- different scales of consideration;
    
- different time horizons;
    
- different criteria of correctness;
    
- struggle over one limited resource or position;
    
- incorrect understanding of a message.
    

Conflict is especially dangerous when at once:

- the reply window is short;
    
- consequences are long-lasting;
    
- the cost of correction is high or infinite;
    
- participants do not have time to check whether they understand the situation the same way.
    

### Link between the two theories

> **Communication is a mechanism of alignment. Conflict is the result of insufficient or impossible alignment.**

But conflict does not always reduce to misunderstanding: people may fully understand each other and still have incompatible goals.
