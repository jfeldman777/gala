
Kant is interested not in the world (as Aristotle was) but in cognition (that is new)
Besides, he separates Sensible cognition from the work of the Understanding.
Space and time are conditions of sensible cognition.
Categories are instruments of the understanding.
Kant derives them from forms of logic (as Aristotle derived his from forms of language)

Here are the categories as given by Kant

1) quantity — quality — measure
2) reality of a property — unreality — limited reality
3) primary–secondary–mutual (substance–accident)(cause–effect)
4) Modality — possible–impossible, existence–nonexistence, necessary–contingent, interaction–mutual influence

The section on sense perception should be handed over to psychology.
The 12 categories are not 12 but more, and they cannot be justified.

Three modality categories:

- **possibility / impossibility**
- **existence / nonexistence**
- necessity / contingency
this is our evaluation of knowledge about things.
