This is the endpaper of my book "The Philosopher on the Beach"; it shows a page from Ramon Llull's manuscript, along with the names of the sins and virtues as Llull framed them.

Virtues
1. Justice
2. Fortitude
3. Temperance
4. Prudence
5. Modesty
6. Patience
7. Compassion

Sins
1. Lust
2. Gluttony
3. Unreliability
4. Greed
5. Envy
6. Wrath
7. Vanity

Let's work out which levels lie behind these words.

Virtues
- Modesty = 1 (a narrowed field of attention)
- Temperance = 2 (a bit wider, but not expanding, and no movement)
- Patience = 3 (this is striving toward a goal slowly but surely, enduring obstacles along the way)
- Justice = 4, since being just means matching the promises and expectations of the given role system
- Prudence = reason = 6
- Compassion = empathy = 8
- Fortitude — not being afraid to pit your own intellectual position against the whole world = 7

Sins
- Lust = 1 (a fixed idea)
- Gluttony = 2 (many objects)
- Greed = 3 (more and more)
- Vanity = 4 = thinking about ranks not yet received and status not yet attained
- Wrath = 5 — instant mobilization to act
- Envy = 6 — like conscience, but conscience grieves over a missed just deed, while envy grieves over a missed chance to come out first
- Unreliability = 7 — an excessive inclination toward innovation

In total, in a table:

| 1   | lust          | modesty     |
| --- | ------------- | ------------ |
| 2   | gluttony      | temperance   |
| 3   | greed         | patience     |
| 4   | vanity        | justice      |
| 5   | wrath         |              |
| 6   | envy          | prudence     |
| 7   | unreliability | fortitude    |
| 8   |               | compassion   |

Question. Why is there no 8 among the sins, and no 5 among the virtues?
Answer. Level 8 is always a blessing.
Level 5 is a threat to level 4.

The Christian faith stands on 4 and 8, which is why this is so.
