Every three years a child is supposed to take a new barrier

Here a mother has brought a three-year-old to daycare.

It is his first time here; he is scared; he clings to his mother. One object in the field of attention. But if this is a healthy child, after a while he pulls away from mother and runs off to play — not with children — with toys. Many objects in the field of attention

That was the barrier of 3 years

## Barrier of 6 years

If you have children of your own and / or you have watched how children grow, you have surely noticed that “learning is limited by maturation.”

Here he has already learned all the letters — and still cannot read a word!!! But do not grieve. The age arrives and the letters assemble themselves into words.

All over the world, between ages 6 and 7, children come to school and all of them read, write, count.

Note that all three of these operations require “control of a linear process.”

Somewhere earlier (at 2–3–4 years) the child moves from one toy — to many toys.

We will call these Levels of the field of attention.

1. One object
2. Many objects
3. One process

And what next?

4 . Many processes?

And how can one see that? Here are two examples from my personal observations. The experiment is reproducible, but to see what I saw you need the theoretical model.

## First observation

I worked in a school twice, both times — two school years in a row.

I was put on substitution across all ages and I have the chance to compare.

Let us compare together. Here I came on substitution to elementary school, grades 1, 2, 3.

I give an assignment — open the textbooks, open the notebooks, write. Everyone writes, silence, only noses sniffle.

And here I am in fifth grade. I give an assignment. I turn to the board — behind me a hum — everyone is discussing what is going on.

How is this connected to the field of attention?

at 6–7 a person sees one process. My notebook, and I write in it. And at 11–12–13 a person sees many processes — here is my notebook, and here is the neighbor’s notebook, and I see both at once.

## Second observation

Lessons are over. I look out the window onto the football field, where first grade, numbering no fewer than ten, plays football against two fifth-graders and loses by a shutout. How so? Like this. One fifth-grader runs around the field with the ball and all the first-graders run after him in a crowd. Having drawn that crowd to the edge of the field, the fifth-grader passes to the center and his friend scores on the goalkeeper. And so about ten times!

How is that even possible? Like this. First-graders can hold one process in the field of attention. And many processes they cannot. And fifth-graders can.

## Third observation

I was present at an American school at a celebration when the entire parallel completing elementary school put on a play. Everyone took part. Their participation in the role system was a pledge that they were ready for the next stage of schooling.

## Summary

3 6 9 12 — every three years the field of attention moves to a new level

The next levels may be reached, or may not

15 — transition to theoretical consciousness, to discussing the picture of the world

18 — inventiveness

21 — understanding of many cultures

I draw the reader’s attention to the fact that age only opens the possibility of solving tasks of the next level. That possibility must be locked in by experience. Experience can be successful or unsuccessful. Successful experience makes levels beloved. An adult returns to beloved levels and avoids the unbeloved ones
