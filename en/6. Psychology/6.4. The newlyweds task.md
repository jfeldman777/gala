In chapter 5.5.2 we took apart the mechanisms of horizontal regulation (there they were called Pressure mechanisms)

Now we can solve the following task.

He and she entered into lawful marriage and decided to move in together onto one living space.
Conflicts are inevitable.
How to minimize them?

I propose the following algorithm of 4 steps along the even levels from the bottom up.

Step 1. Level 2 — space, boundary.

One must agree about space
this is mine, this is yours, this is shared. On my territory I manage — I do not report. On yours — you; on the shared — consensus.

Remark. In N. G. Chernyshevsky’s *What Is to Be Done* there is this thought:
For family life two adults need three rooms.

Step 2. Level 4. Role system.

One must agree about roles, that is, about mutual promises and obligations.
If the young people come from different family regimes, this is a very hard operation

Step 3. Level 6. Theoretical thinking.

One can discuss the picture of the world, principles of morality, each other’s motives and those of other people, etc.

Step 4. Level 8. Empathy.

Give a person not what they ask for, but what they need.

End of the algorithm.

If, climbing the levels together, you see that your partner has fallen behind you — prepare for divorce. But if you have reached the same step together — you will live long and happily..
