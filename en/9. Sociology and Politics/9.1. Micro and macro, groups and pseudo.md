Macro-sociology is the study of groups and pseudo-groups in the society of the present and the past.
Micro-sociology is the study of the role system on a public stage. An example — long-lived games 9.2. There are other examples.

I define groups as communities with a single standard of demonstration. It is known what one must demonstrate, what one is forbidden to demonstrate.
In groups there are necessarily people who have the right to admit outsiders into the community and, conversely, to exclude from the community.

In pseudo-groups people are united by researchers according to some trait.
A person may not even know that they have been included and counted.
The paradox is that even in pseudo-groups there are their own standards; it is known what is usually demonstrated and what is not.

The difference is that in groups incorrect members are expelled by leaders or moral authorities of the group, and in pseudo-groups they are excluded by researchers as NOT TYPICAL.

Otherwise, if one considers only the group and its standard, then a pseudo-group and its standard behave exactly the same.

Let there be X = (a pseudo-group and its standard). And there is a group A and its standard coinciding with X; then A is a typical representative of X.

For example A — the pupils of school №1 of St. Petersburg, and X — all schoolchildren of St. Petersburg; then it is possible that A are typical representatives of X.
