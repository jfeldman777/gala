### 1. Political slogans are deliberately polysemous

This is indeed a widespread phenomenon.

For example:

- “liberty”;
    
- “justice”;
    
- “the people”;
    
- “democracy”;
    
- “traditional values.”
    

Each group puts its own meaning into them. That is precisely why such slogans can unite very different forces.

This thesis is hard to dispute.
