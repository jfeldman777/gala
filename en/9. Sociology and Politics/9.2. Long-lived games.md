People invent games throughout the history of humankind. Some games live for centuries (like chess). Others die unnoticed, but quickly.

What do all long-lived games have in common?

To play, one must work at level X; to win, one must work at level X+1

It turns out to be a pump:
children, growing up, enter the game to show that they have already grown up. Then they begin to win. Then they grow up and leave, but others come. That is how all long-lived games are arranged.

Lotto = 12
Dominoes = 23
bicycle race = 34
football = 45
chess = 56
....
