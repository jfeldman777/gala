Let us consider the most studied example.
France on the eve of 1789.
The country is ruled by the king and two estates — the nobility and the clergy.

What is an estate?
It is a corporation tuned to solving certain tasks and having a standard level of its member (and character traits standard for that level)

Nevertheless, this corporation has a standard level tied to its origin.
The nobility were originally officers. level 3.
The clergy were originally preachers, level 4.

It is precisely an alliance of two neighboring levels that is most probable according to the rules of elementary conflict.
And from the point of view of info-logical pyramids two neighboring levels superbly cover all channels of control and society works as a single organism

Further we will show that European states are arranged — at least until the 20th century — in this way — an alliance of neighbors, and this gives them strength and at the same time plasticity. The Russian state is arranged differently — about that later.

That is, in Europe two neighboring levels are political power.
For example nobility 3 and clergy 4.
And the economic regime? another level is needed from below = the peasantry. (2)

And why do we think that the next epoch will again have a state of two consecutive levels? Let us consider what Martin Luther brought into politics.

The reforms he proposed are directly tied to his “under-the-table” political program — to reorient the clergy (4) from the old ally (the nobility) to a new ally = the bourgeoisie (5). Luther’s victory in the North German lands meant that precisely here the new epoch had already arrived.

What measures did Luther propose and from where is it clear that they are aimed directly at changing the ally?

1. Translation of the Bible into the vernacular
2. Asceticism of the church and its self-government
3. Closing of monasteries and transfer of their land holdings to the state.
In order.
A nobleman is not obliged to read — his duty is war. A trained priest reads the prayer for him. At the same time, going to war he knows he will plunder and kill — and then someone must pray away his earthly sins. For that there is a priest.
But a priest will not do this for free — and need not. The nobleman will return from war with trophies and donate them to the church — but the priest will not eat everything himself — a significant part will go to the church — that is why the church has such expensive interiors.
And when the nobleman grows old and can no longer fight, and becomes a veteran, sick and wounded, he will be taken into a monastery and will live out his short days there.

A bourgeois, first, can read — without that one cannot sign a contract.
Second, he did not plunder, he traded and remembers every kopeck — there is nothing to donate to the temple. And even those kopecks that the community will collect (without Roman financing) one wants to spend wisely and account for before the community.
That is second and third. And fourth, he intends to die at home among children and grandchildren and not in a monastery on others’ bread.

That is, the Reformation is a transfer of power from 34 to 45. And the economic base of the epoch is the labor of new townspeople (lumpen), only just come from village to city, their level 3 (those remaining in the village — level 2).
