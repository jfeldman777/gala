This is a separate model of the state: **a stable force backend + a replaceable ideological and social facade**.

## Schema

### Backend

The deep construction of power:

- **531** — autocracy, the force vertical;
    
- **7531** — the same vertical plus myth-making, censorship and control of culture.
    

It changes rarely.

### Facade

The public image of an epoch: which qualities are declared modern, desirable and worthy of imitation.

By your markup:

|Period|Facade|
|---|--:|
|futurism, Mayakovsky|**4567**|
|Stalin’s engineering projects|**3456**|
|Brezhnev moralizing|**2468**|
|small business of perestroika|**2345**|
|Putin’s archaization|**1234**|

That is, the facade sets for society:

- the language of self-description;
    
- heroes;
    
- admissible dreams;
    
- the educational standard;
    
- the way of justifying power.
    

But the real mechanism of transmitting an order from top to bottom is preserved.

## Where this model is strong

It explains why in Russia slogans, cultural styles and the image of the future can change sharply, while the arrangement of power is reproduced.

For example:

```text
new facade
↓
new words, heroes, symbols
↓
old channel of control
↓
the order passes from top to bottom
```

This also allows one to distinguish:

- **a change of the regime of control**;
    
- **a change of the way it is presented to the population**.
    

## Weak point

So far your numeric estimates of facades look plausible, but not proven.

For example, the Stalin era simultaneously contained:

- engineering design;
    
- mass mobilization;
    
- archaic subordination;
    
- myth-making;
    
- hard control of culture.
    

Therefore **3456** may describe the official image of construction, but not the whole regime.

To check, each facade needs independent traits:

1. who is the hero of the epoch;
    
2. what the youth is taught;
    
3. what behavior is publicly encouraged;
    
4. which levels it is forbidden to demonstrate;
    
5. how the real transmission of an order is arranged.
    

Then one can strictly show:

> **the backend remained 531/7531, and the facade changed from one quartet to another.**

**Confidence:** medium, about 75%. As an analytical construction the model is strong; concrete markups of epochs require a separate historical analysis.
